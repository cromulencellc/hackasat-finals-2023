Moonlighter Satellite

Moonlighter is equipped with a camera and attitude control system for capturing imagery of the Earth's surface. With provided pointing commands, we can capture imagery of specific areas of interest.

Ground Systems

Suporting Moonlighter's on-orit operations is a network of ground systems. From task ingestion, satellite communications, and data processing, our ground systems handle tasking across multiple users.

Tasking Interface

Our tasking interface allows users to reserve orbital operations windows and schedule thier mission-specific tasking. Users are able to upload scripts, point the spacecraft, and capture images for downlink. Window Viewer also provides real-time info on currently reserved windows and scheduled tasking.

Challenges

Solve the challenges!

Script Kiddie

Moonlighter is running a scripting engine that allows users to run custom Lua scripts. These scripts can communicate with the flight software and are used to complete complex operations and challenges. As an introductory challenge, teams must show that they can upload a basic script and schedule it to be run.

Shutterbug

Moonlighter is equipped with a camera. Teams are tasked to take a picture of any target from a set of targets spaced around the globe using the Tasking Interface. Points are awarded for successfully aiming the camera, snapping a picture, and downlinking the image.

Target Deck

Unintended Bug

Moonlighter implements a geofence that prevents players from taking pictures of certain locations. Teams must figure out how to circumvent the geofence in order to take a picture of a protected location. Points are awarded for taking the picture and also for downlinking the image.

Geofence

Iron Bank

Teams get access to a binary for a script security service running on Moonligher called Iron Bank. Players can hack the service and trigger it download the flag.

Christmas In August

Moonlighter is in a known orbit with an inclination of 51.6 degrees. That means its latitude will always be between +/- 51.6 degrees. If teams are able to get Moonlighter above 80 degrees they will get a flag.
